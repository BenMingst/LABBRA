About the LABBRA System

The LABBRA System is an innovative, advanced impact detection technology designed to protect pedestrians by reducing response times in emergency situations.

With a vision detection system powered by edge computing devices and wide-angle cameras, it helps emergency responders act quickly and efficiently, potentially saving lives. Named after six of the UCF students (London, Alexandria, Brooke, Brandon, Reuel, Austin) who tragically lost their lives while walking arround campus, the LABBRA System honors their memory by improving pedestrian safety through cutting-edge technology.

The Problem

On average in 2024, two pedestrians died every day on Florida's streets, totaling 686 deaths. This number continues to rise both statewide and nationally.

I'm reminded of these tragedies every day when I cross the road next to Brooke Dawkins' memorial. As a student, I've witnessed many close calls near campus. While some proactive measures exist, current solutions are costly, hard to scale, and preventative. To truly save lives, we need a reactive system that responds in real-time.
